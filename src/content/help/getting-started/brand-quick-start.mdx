# Brand Quick Start Guide

Launch your brand presence in BakedBot quickly, with the minimum setup needed to publish products, connect retail partners, and start using AI workflows.

## What you'll set up

- Your brand profile and visual identity
- Your core product catalog
- Retail partner and fulfillment context
- A first campaign or outreach workflow
- Basic analytics and inbox monitoring

<Callout type="tip">
Start lean. You only need enough structure for customers and retail partners to recognize your brand, find your products, and act on your first campaign.
</Callout>

## Step 1: Complete your brand profile

Open your dashboard and fill in the basics:

- Brand name and public description
- Logo and brand colors
- Website and contact info
- Target markets or states you operate in

This gives Craig, Smokey, and your public brand pages the context they need to generate accurate content and recommendations.

## Step 2: Add your first products

Use whichever path is fastest for your team:

- Manual product entry for a small starter catalog
- CSV import if you already have structured product data
- POS or partner sync if inventory already lives in another system

Focus first on:

- Best sellers
- Hero SKUs
- Seasonal or promotional products
- Accurate images, categories, and pricing

## Step 3: Connect your retail reality

For each retail or dispensary relationship, confirm:

- Which partners carry your products
- Which markets need outreach or support
- Which contacts own buyer or merchandising decisions
- What fulfillment path customers should follow

<Callout type="info">
BakedBot works best when your product, partner, and CRM context line up. Even a lightweight CRM import is enough to power smarter drafts and outreach suggestions.
</Callout>

## Step 4: Turn on your first workflow

Pick one outcome for your first week:

- Generate a campaign draft
- Build a product page or carousel
- Launch an outreach batch from CRM data
- Review analytics or competitive intel for one market

Avoid enabling everything at once. A single clean workflow gives you faster feedback and easier debugging.

## Step 5: Review the command centers

Before you consider setup complete, check:

- Inbox: agent outputs, drafts, and approvals
- Analytics: top-line activity and product traction
- Help Center: onboarding docs for your next setup area
- Outreach or Campaigns: first execution flow

## Recommended first-week checklist

1. Publish 5 to 20 products
2. Confirm your public brand profile looks correct
3. Run one AI-assisted marketing or outreach draft
4. Review approvals before sending anything customer-facing
5. Check that analytics and inbox updates are appearing normally
