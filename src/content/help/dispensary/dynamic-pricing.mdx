## Publishing Prices to Your Menu

### What is "Publish to Menu"?

When you create pricing rules, they exist in the BakedBot system but **don't automatically appear on your public menu**. The "Publish to Menu" feature applies all your active pricing rules to your live menu in one click, making price changes visible to customers immediately.

**How it works:**

1. You create pricing rules (clearance, happy hour, competitor matching, etc.)
2. Click **"Publish to Menu"** in the Publish to Menu tab
3. BakedBot evaluates all active rules against your product catalog
4. Products matching rule conditions get updated prices
5. Discount badges appear next to prices on your public menu
6. Original prices are preserved for easy reverting

### When to Publish

**Best times to publish:**

- ‚úÖ **Monday mornings** - Start the week with fresh promotions
- ‚úÖ **Off-peak hours (9-11am, 2-4pm)** - Minimize customer confusion from mid-session price changes
- ‚úÖ **Before major events** - Pre-plan 4/20, local events, holidays
- ‚úÖ **After creating new rules** - Test new rules on live menu before committing to POS sync

**Avoid publishing:**

- ‚ùå **During peak hours** - Customers may be mid-checkout when prices change
- ‚ùå **Without testing first** - Always preview in Analytics tab before publishing
- ‚ùå **Too frequently** - Constant price changes erode customer trust

<Callout type="warning">
**Important:** Publishing to your BakedBot menu is separate from syncing to your POS system. If you want dynamic pricing in both places, you need to:
1. Publish to Menu (makes prices visible on bakedbot.ai/yourstore)
2. Sync to POS (creates discounts in your Alleaves/Dutchie/Jane system)
</Callout>

### How to Publish Prices

**Step-by-step:**

1. Navigate to **Dashboard ‚Üí Dynamic Pricing**
2. Click the **"Publish to Menu"** tab
3. Review the summary:

5. Wait 10-30 seconds for batch processing
6. Success message shows:

   - Total savings (e.g., "$1,245.67 in customer savings")

### Reverting Prices

**Need to undo?** No problem. The "Revert All Prices" button instantly restores all products to their original prices.

**When to revert:**

- üîß **Rule malfunction** - Discount applied too aggressively
- üìä **Negative customer feedback** - Price changes received poorly
- üéØ **Promotion ended** - Return to standard pricing after sale

**How to revert:**

1. Go to **Publish to Menu** tab
2. Click **"Revert All Prices"** button
3. Confirm the action (this is irreversible)
4. All products return to original prices immediately
5. Discount badges removed from menu

</Callout>

## Creating Your First Pricing Rule

### Step 1: Choose a Strategy

**4 pricing strategies:**

1. **Clearance** - Move aging inventory before expiration
2. **Competitive** - Match or beat competitor prices
3. **Time-Based** - Happy hour, weekend specials, seasonal promos
4. **Dynamic** - Category-specific or segment-based pricing

### Step 2: Set Conditions

**Condition Types:**

| Condition | When to Use | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Inventory Age** | Clear old stock | Products 45+ days old |
| **Competitor Price** | Stay competitive | When 15%+ above market avg |
| **Time of Day** | Drive off-peak traffic | Weekdays 2-5pm |
| **Day of Week** | Weekend specials | Friday-Sunday |
| **Category** | Promote specific products | All flower products |
| **Stock Level** | Urgent clearance | Less than 5 units |
| **Traffic Level** | Surge pricing | Peak hours (controversial!) |

<Callout type="warning">
**Multiple conditions = AND logic:** All conditions must be met for the rule to apply. Use multiple rules if you need OR logic.
</Callout>

### Step 3: Configure Price Adjustment

**Percentage Discount** (recommended):
- Set discount: 10%, 20%, 30%, etc.
- Min price: Prevent selling below cost ($5.00 minimum)
- Max discount: System enforces 40% maximum

**Fixed Discount**:
- Dollar amount off: $5 off, $10 off, etc.
- Use for specific promotions

### Step 4: Set Priority

**Priority determines rule order (100 = highest):**

- **90-100**: Urgent clearance (expiring soon)
- **70-89**: Competitor matching (market-driven)
- **50-69**: Time-based promotions (happy hour, weekends)
- **20-49**: Category deals (ongoing)
- **1-19**: Default discounts (loyalty, new customers)

**Why priority matters:**
Only the **highest priority matching rule** applies to each product. If multiple rules match, the one with the highest priority wins.

### Rule Not Applying

**Check:**
- ‚úÖ Rule is active (toggle ON)
- ‚úÖ Conditions are met (inventory age threshold, time range)
- ‚úÖ Priority is high enough (not overridden by another rule)
- ‚úÖ Product matches conditions (category, stock level)
- ‚úÖ Min price not blocking discount (product price > min price)

### Discount Not Syncing to POS

**Fix:**
- Verify Alleaves integration active
- Check API credentials valid
- Review integration logs (Settings ‚Üí Integrations)
- Contact support if persistent

### Competitor Data Missing

**Reasons:**
- Ezal not enabled for your market (contact support)
- Competitor menus not scraped yet (24-48hr initial)
- Product name mismatch (fuzzy matching may fail)
- Data freshness (check last updated timestamp)

### Price Too Low / Too High

**Adjust:**
- Increase minimum price (Rules tab ‚Üí Edit ‚Üí Min Price)
- Lower discount percentage (30% ‚Üí 20%)
- Adjust priority (prevent aggressive clearance from always winning)

## FAQ

**Q: Can I stack multiple discounts?**
A: No, only one rule applies per product (highest priority). Create a single rule with combined discount if needed.

**Q: Will surge pricing upset customers?**
A: Yes, probably. Use surge pricing (negative discounts) very carefully. Customers dislike paying more during peak hours.

**Q: How often does pricing update?**
A: Real-time for rule changes, 5-minute cache for inventory/competitor data, instant sync to POS.

**Q: Can I override a rule for specific products?**
A: Yes, use product ID conditions or disable the rule temporarily. Manual POS discounts override dynamic pricing.

**Q: Do I need Ezal for dynamic pricing?**
A: No, Ezal is only required for competitor price matching rules. Time-based, clearance, and category rules work without it.

**Q: What if I don't have Alleaves POS?**
A: Dynamic pricing requires POS integration for inventory age tracking. We support Alleaves, Dutchie, and Jane. Contact support for other systems.
