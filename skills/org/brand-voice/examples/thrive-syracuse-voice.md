# Brand Voice Example: Thrive Syracuse

**Brand slug:** `thrivesyracuse`
**Channel:** Email + SMS
**Voice axis:** Warm & community-forward / conversational

---

## Approved Language Patterns

**Personality:** Knowledgeable neighborhood dispensary. Welcoming, approachable, rooted in the Syracuse
community. Not corporate, not aggressively hype. People come in and feel like regulars.

**Sentence style:** Short to medium. Direct. Warm but not over-the-top.

**Emoji usage:** Occasional — 1 per message max. 💜 for closings, 🌿 for product moments.

---

## On-Brand Copy Examples

### Email subject lines
- "Welcome to Thrive, [name] 💜"
- "Your first visit points are waiting"
- "Something new just landed on Erie Blvd"

### SMS body (160 chars)
- "Hey [name]! You've got 150 Thrive points — that's $5 off your next visit. Stop by soon. Reply STOP to opt out."

### In-app / chat
- "Hey! Looking for something to wind down tonight? I can point you to a few things that are really popular right now."

---

## Off-Brand Patterns to Avoid

| Avoid | Why | Use Instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| "HUGE SALE!!!" | Too aggressive, not Thrive's register | "This week only — stop by and save" |
| "proven to relax you" | Medical claim | "customers love it for winding down" |
| Corporate jargon ("leverage", "synergy") | Feels cold | Plain language |
| Over-explaining loyalty math | Loses people | "100 points = $5 back. Simple." |
| "certified organic" (unverified) | False claim | "lab-tested, NY-licensed" |

---

## Voice Alignment Checklist

Before approving copy for Thrive:
- [ ] Would a friendly neighborhood staffer say this?
- [ ] Does it feel like Erie Blvd, not a national chain?
- [ ] Is loyalty math concrete and accurate?
- [ ] No medical claims, even casual ones?
- [ ] SMS includes opt-out / email includes unsubscribe?
